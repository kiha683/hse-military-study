Three Interpretations of Long-Range Strike

Interpretation 1: Extended Stand-Off Defense

Strike enemy ships attempting to invade Japan from beyond their threat range ‚Äî essentially an extension of close-in defense. This interpretation stays closest to traditional policy.

Strike capabilities extending to the East China Sea, Sea of Japan, Chinese mainland, and North Korea. This includes anti-ship strikes against Chinese vessels far from Japan AND land-attack strikes against military targets on the continent. The 2032 goal reflects this interpretation. –®–∏—Ä–æ–∫–æ–º–∞—Å—à—Ç–∞–±–Ω–∞—è —É–¥–∞—Ä–Ω–∞—è —Å–ø–æ—Å–æ–±–Ω–æ—Å—Ç—å

Paper III ‚Äî Section 2
From Force-Building Focus to Operational Focus

Chijiwa identifies a second fundamental shift. Under the Basic Defense Force Concept, "independent response to limited small-scale aggression" was NOT an operational plan ‚Äî it was a force-building benchmark. As former Defense Counselor Shiota Akira testified: "The Outline says the JSDF will fight independently against limited small-scale attacks. I suffered endlessly explaining that distinction."

This meant postwar Japan engaged in "force-building for the sake of force-building" ‚Äî without deeply considering how those forces would actually be used in combat.
