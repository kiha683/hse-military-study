Introduction
The Science of Strategy: Origin & Purpose

The Science of Military Strategy 战略学 Военная стратегия is the theoretical system that studies the overall guiding laws for the use and construction of military forces. Its main task is to reveal the essence, characteristics, and basic principles of strategy — providing systematic theoretical guidance for the formulation and implementation of national military strategy.

The book traces strategic thought from ancient China — where Sun Tzu's Art of War 孙子兵法 Искусство войны Сунь-Цзы remains "the earliest and most influential strategic theory work in the world" — through Western traditions (Clausewitz, Jomini, Liddell Hart) to modern Chinese strategic thinking under Mao Zedong, Deng Xiaoping, Xi Jinping, and the concept of "informatized" warfare 信息化战争 Информатизированная война.

The book identifies three research domains of modern Chinese strategy:

1. Strategic basic theory — concepts, elements, characteristics, and operating laws of strategy

2. Strategic application theory — guidance for using military force (war and non-war operations)

3. Force construction theory — building military power for informatized warfare

Five Frontiers of Modern Strategic Research

The 2020 revision identifies key expanding frontiers:

1
Overseas Expansion
China's national interests now extend to "vast areas overseas" — trade, resources, strategic channels, personnel. Заморская экспансия
2
New Domains
Space, cyberspace, deep sea, polar regions, biology, and artificial intelligence as contested military domains. Новые области
3
Non-War Operations
Peacekeeping, evacuation, escort, counter-terrorism — increasingly prominent ways of using military force. Невоенные операции
4
Intelligentization
AI-driven warfare as the next revolution after informatization. "The intelligence characteristics of war are increasing." Интеллектуализация
5
Civil-Military Fusion
Deep integration of national civilian technology with military capability — a core Xi Jinping initiative. Военно-гражданская интеграция

Chapter 1 · 第一章
Introduction to Strategy 战略概论
"Strategy is the overall planning and guidance for the use and construction of military forces." Стратегия — это общее планирование и руководство использованием и строительством вооружённых сил.

Definition & Three Core Elements

The Chinese definition of strategy has evolved significantly. The current formulation: "Strategy is the overall planning and guidance for the use and construction of military forces" 战略是军事力量运用和建设的总体筹划和指导. This deliberately encompasses both war operations and non-war military operations — a critical distinction from earlier definitions focused solely on warfare.

Every strategy contains three indispensable elements:

目标
Strategic Goal Стратегическая цель
The basic requirement the state sets for military conflict — "what to do." The starting point AND end point of strategy formulation and implementation.
方针
Strategic Policy Стратегический курс
The guiding principles and general plan — "how to do it." Must be based on scientific assessment of subjective and objective conditions of both sides.
手段
Strategic Means Стратегические средства
The military forces and methods used to achieve goals — "with what to do it." Includes both military and non-military instruments of power.

Classification of Strategies

Strategies are classified along multiple axes: by nature (offensive vs. defensive), by scope (comprehensive war vs. local war локальная война), by duration (quick-decision vs. protracted затяжная война), and by domain (nuclear, conventional, informatized). The book notes that China's strategic system follows a four-level hierarchy similar to the U.S. model: National Security Strategy → National Defense Strategy → Military Strategy → Theater Strategy стратегия театра военных действий.

Five Basic Characteristics of Strategy

Characteristic 1
Political Nature Политическая природа
Strategy is fundamentally a political instrument. War is the continuation of politics, and strategy serves political goals.
Characteristic 2
Holistic Perspective Целостный характер
Strategy addresses the "overall situation" (全局) — it must integrate all aspects, parts, and stages of military conflict.
Characteristic 3
Adversarial Nature Противоборство
Strategy exists in relation to opponents. It is formulated in response to specific threats and is inherently confrontational.
Characteristic 4
Predictive Character Предвидение
Strategy requires foresight — anticipating future developments and preparing in advance for multiple scenarios.
Characteristic 5
Relative Stability Относительная стабильность
Strategy must be both stable (providing consistent guidance) and adaptive (changing with circumstances). No strategy is immutable.

Strategic Thinking of Contemporary China

The book devotes special attention to three pillars of Chinese strategic thought:

1. People's War (人民战争) Народная война

Remains a foundational concept, but adapted for modern conditions: the "mass basis" has broadened beyond peasants to include all social groups. The forms of People's War now include information warfare, economic mobilization, and civilian technology integration.

2. Active Defense (积极防御) Активная оборона

Active defense is China's declared military strategy since the founding of the PRC. It means: strategically defensive but operationally and tactically offensive. China will not strike first strategically, but once attacked, will respond with vigorous counterattack. Critically, the text notes this "does not exclude the flexibility of first strike" at the operational and tactical level.

3. Xi Jinping's Military Thought Военная мысль Си Цзиньпина

Xi's "Strong Army Dream" (强军梦) represents the current guiding ideology: mechanization and informatization fusion, preparation for war as the core mission, military-civilian integration as national strategy, and the goal of building a "world-class military" by mid-century.

Influencing Factors

The book identifies five factors that determine strategy: national interests национальные интересы, national defense policy оборонная политика, security environment среда безопасности, comprehensive national power совокупная мощь государства, and strategic culture стратегическая культура.

Chapter 2 · 第二章
Strategic Judgment 战略判断

Strategic judgment Стратегическое суждение is the starting point of all strategic operations — the process of analyzing and assessing the security situation facing the country. The book identifies it as requiring three qualities: comprehensiveness, foresight, and accuracy.

What Must Be Judged

The strategic environment assessment covers: the international strategic landscape, the security threats facing the nation, the balance of forces between potential adversaries, technological trends, and the political/economic environment. The key judgment is: what is the main threat, and when might it materialize?

Requirements for Sound Judgment

Holistic Analysis Комплексный анализ
Must consider political, economic, military, diplomatic, scientific, and cultural dimensions simultaneously. Cannot rely on any single factor.
Forward-Looking Vision Перспективное видение
Must anticipate trends, not merely react to current conditions. "Those who foresee change and act first will prevail."
Materialist Dialectics Материалистическая диалектика
Must analyze contradictions objectively, avoid both overconfidence and pessimism, and identify the "principal contradiction" (主要矛盾) in each situation.
Chapters 3–6
Decision → Planning → Implementation → Evaluation

Chapters 3 through 6 detail the five-link strategic operation cycle Стратегический цикл — the sequential process by which strategy moves from judgment to execution:

The Five-Link Strategic Operation Cycle — 战略运行五环节

Chapter 3: Strategic Decision-Making 战略决策 Стратегическое решение

Strategic decision-making determines strategic goals, policies, and means. Key requirements: decisions must be scientific (evidence-based), democratic (incorporating expert counsel), and decisive (not paralyzed by uncertainty). The book emphasizes that "only leadership at the highest level can make strategic decisions" — this is a core top-down principle.

Chapter 4: Strategic Planning 战略筹划 Стратегическое планирование

Planning translates decisions into actionable programs. It encompasses: force deployment, resource allocation, timeline construction, and contingency preparation. The text stresses three requirements: systematic thinking, scientific methodology (including simulation and wargaming), and flexibility for adaptation.

Chapter 5: Strategy Implementation 战略实施 Стратегическая реализация

Implementation principles include: firm adherence to strategic goals while maintaining tactical flexibility; maintaining strategic initiative; concentration of forces at decisive points; and the critical importance of strategic reserves стратегические резервы. The text devotes particular attention to the principle of "you fight your way, I'll fight my way" 你打你的，我打我的 — fighting on one's own terms rather than being drawn into the enemy's preferred mode of warfare.

Chapter 6: Strategic Evaluation 战略评估 Стратегическая оценка

Assessment covers: evaluation of the strategic environment, evaluation of one's own military capabilities, and evaluation of strategy implementation effectiveness. Methods include: quantitative analysis, simulation, wargaming, expert consultation, and historical case study. The key point is that evaluation must be continuous, not merely post-hoc.

Chapter 7 · 第七章
Prevention & Handling of Military Crisis 军事危机的预防和处置

Military crisis Военный кризис is defined as a state of intense confrontation between nations or political groups that creates high risk of military conflict. Crises are characterized by their progressiveness and suddenness — they develop gradually but can erupt rapidly.

Crisis Prevention

The book outlines four preparatory measures: pre-assessment (risk identification), pre-planning (contingency development), pre-arrangement (force positioning), and pre-action (readiness enhancement). Key tools include: computer simulation, sand table exercises настольные учения, wargaming 兵棋推演, and scenario response training.

Crisis Handling Principles

Control Scope Контроль масштаба
Prevent escalation beyond the original issue. Avoid turning small conflicts into large wars, civil wars into foreign wars, or single opponents into multiple opponents.
Control Intensity Контроль интенсивности
The reaction cannot be lower than the target (appearing weak) nor exceed the target (causing escalation). Proportionality is essential.
Control Rhythm Контроль ритма
Control the speed of crisis development — rapid escalation can overwhelm decision-making; too slow a response may appear hesitant.
Legal Framework Правовая основа
"Handle the crisis within a legal framework." When international law conflicts with national law, national law takes priority.

Notable precedent cited: China's 2008 decision to deploy naval vessels for escort missions in the waters of Somalia is described as "a successful example of leading the crisis" — demonstrating willingness to use military force overseas when national interests require it.

Chapter 8 · 第八章
Strategic Deterrence 战略威慑
"Strategic deterrence is aimed at the opponent's psychology, cognition and decision-making system." Стратегическое сдерживание направлено на психологию, сознание и систему принятия решений противника.

Strategic deterrence Стратегическое сдерживание is defined as using military strength or the threat of its use to compel an adversary to abandon hostile actions. Compared with actual combat, deterrence has "lower intensity, lower cost, and greater room for maneuver" — it can directly achieve political goals without fighting.

Types of Strategic Deterrence

Nuclear
Nuclear Deterrence Ядерное сдерживание
Three levels: maximum (massive retaliation), medium ("sufficient and effective" — China's stated approach), and minimum. China pursues "lean and effective" (精干有效) nuclear forces — fewer warheads but reliable second-strike capability.
Nuclear
Conventional
Conventional Deterrence Обычное сдерживание
Using conventional military superiority — precision strike capability, naval presence, air power — to demonstrate ability to inflict unacceptable costs.
Conventional
Space
Space Deterrence Космическое сдерживание
"Strategically prominent with great deterrent effect." Methods include interference, destruction, and capability demonstration. Highly dependent on space technology credibility.
Space
Cyber
Information/Cyber Deterrence Информационное сдерживание
Demonstrating ability to disrupt adversary's information systems, networks, and command architecture through cyber operations.
Cyber
People's War
People's War Deterrence Сдерживание народной войной
"Fully demonstrates and exerts the great power of People's War to deter the enemy." Historical precedent: deterring foreign invasion after PRC founding through demonstrated national mobilization capability.
Mobilization

Offensive deterrence Наступательное сдерживание — threatening to initiate destructive action if adversary crosses red lines.

Defensive deterrence Оборонительное сдерживание — demonstrating ability to deny adversary's objectives and absorb attacks.

Superior deterrence — from a position of military advantage.

Balanced deterrence — between roughly equal forces (e.g., Cold War US-USSR).

Inferior deterrence Сдерживание слабейшей стороной — the weaker side using asymmetric capabilities (e.g., nuclear weapons by a smaller power against a larger conventional threat). This is China's historical position.

Methods of Deterrence

1
Show Advanced Weapons
Demonstrating nuclear, precision-guided, space, or "new concept" weapons to advertise destructive capability. Демонстрация оружия
2
Adjust Military Deployment
Maneuvering forces toward a specific direction, forward-deploying new weapons, expanding readiness levels. Передислокация войск
3
Restrictive Military Operations
Military exercises, test firings, no-fly zones, close reconnaissance patrols, threatening sea/air lines of communication. Ограничительные операции
4
Form Deterrent Posture
Creating visible state of readiness that communicates: "if you act, the consequences will be severe." Формирование позиции сдерживания

Chapter 9 · 第九章
Military Conflict in New Domains 新型领域军事斗争
"The new domains are not only an important domain of military conflict, but also an integral part of international political struggle." Новые области — это не только важная сфера военного конфликта, но и неотъемлемая часть международной политической борьбы.

This is one of the most significant chapters — identifying six new contested domains beyond traditional land, sea, and air:

Six New Domains of Military Conflict — 新型领域军事斗争

§1 Space 太空 Космическое пространство

Space provides "information support for the implementation of diversified tasks" — wide coverage, strong timeliness, no border restrictions. Space military conflict is global and united; any space facility must obey orbital mechanics; motion is the basic attribute of all objects in space. Strategic guidance: actively respond to space security threats, effectively maintain space strategic interests. The text explicitly discusses space deterrence and "space offensive and defensive operations."

§2 Cyberspace 网络空间 Киберпространство

Cyberspace is described as a "man-made field" as important as land, sea, air, and space. Military conflict here is: unbounded in time and space, highly flexible, blurring offense/defense, difficult to attribute, and low-cost/high-effectiveness. The Stuxnet incident is cited as "a classic case" demonstrating peacetime cyber operations as a precursor to war. Key operations: cyber reconnaissance, network attack, network defense, electromagnetic warfare.

§3 Deep Sea 深海 Глубоководные районы

The deep-sea environment presents "easy to attack, difficult to defend" characteristics. Submarine stealth and mine warfare remain devastatingly effective — the book cites cases of old Soviet submarines penetrating U.S. carrier defenses. Submarine-launched ballistic missiles with 12,000 km range provide second-strike capability. China aims to develop underwater autonomous systems and protect deep-sea infrastructure (cables, pipelines).

§4 Polar Regions 极地 Полярные регионы

The Arctic is highlighted for: strategic shipping routes (reducing distance from Asia to Europe by 40%), vast energy reserves (12.275 billion tons of oil, 47.22 trillion cubic meters of natural gas), and military positioning. China frames its polar interests within "common property of all mankind" while actively building icebreaker and research capabilities.

§5 Biological Domain 生物领域 Биологическая сфера

The book explicitly states: "The great development and major breakthroughs in biotechnology will lead the new trend of military conflict development, causing profound changes in the form of warfare." Biotechnology can "directly kill people, paralyze combat effectiveness on a large scale or accurately." The text references SARS (2003) and COVID-19 (2020) as demonstrating catastrophic potential. Traditional geopolitical frontiers "are far from being able to effectively protect" against biological threats — a "new frontier of biotechnology defense" must be constructed.

§6 Intelligent/AI Domain 智能领域 Область искусственного интеллекта

"Innovative applications of artificial intelligence are gestating new forms of warfare." AI is reshaping all aspects of the military — from autonomous weapons systems to intelligent decision support. The text emphasizes: seize the initiative in AI military development, strengthen "original design" to avoid being attacked by opponents, and develop theoretical frameworks for intelligent warfare before capabilities mature.

Chapter 10 · 第十章
War Planning 战争筹划
"Focus on innovating war and combat planning, closely follow the evolution of war patterns and combat methods." — Xi Jinping «Сосредоточиться на инновационном планировании войны, внимательно следить за эволюцией форм и методов боевых действий» — Си Цзиньпин

Characteristics of Informatized Local War

The book identifies six defining characteristics of the wars China expects to fight:

1
Information Dominance
Seizing information superiority is the primary goal and prerequisite for all other forms of control. Информационное превосходство
2
System Confrontation
War is system vs. system, not platform vs. platform. Combat effectiveness comes from networked integration. Системное противоборство
3
Joint Operations
Multi-domain integrated joint operations are the "basic combat form." All services fight as one system. Совместные операции
4
Precision Strike
Precision-guided munitions enable surgical destruction of critical nodes. "Hitting one point, shaking the whole situation." Высокоточные удары
5
Political Constraints
Wars are limited in scope, duration, and geography. Political objectives constrain military operations at every level. Политические ограничения
6
Accelerating Intelligence
AI and autonomous systems are increasingly characterizing the battlefield. "Intelligentized" (智能化) features are growing. Интеллектуализация

War Planning Components

War planning encompasses eight major components: war determination, operation design, strategic deployment, strategic coordination, overall strategic guarantees, war mobilization, political work planning, and information mobilization информационная мобилизация. The text emphasizes that mobilization in the information age requires "timely and accurate" timing — too early reveals intent, too late loses initiative.

The Eight Elements of War Planning

Element 1
War Determination Определение войны
Strategic assessment of whether war should be fought, against whom, when, and at what scale. "Unless the country is in an extremely critical situation..."
Element 2
Operation Design Замысел операции
Conceptual framework for how the war will be fought — main directions, key targets, force allocation, and phase structure.
Element 3
Strategic Deployment Стратегическое развёртывание
Physical positioning of forces — theater commands, echelons, reserves, support infrastructure.
Element 4
Strategic Coordination Стратегическая координация
Synchronization across services, theaters, and civilian/military organizations. Increasingly important in joint warfare.
Element 5
Strategic Guarantees Стратегическое обеспечение
Logistics, intelligence, communications, equipment, and transportation support at the strategic level.
Element 6
War Mobilization Военная мобилизация
Transforming war potential into military strength: manpower, industry, economy, technology, information, and transportation mobilization.
Chapter 11 · 第十一章
War Operations 战争行动

This chapter details ten types of strategic war operations — the specific military actions that constitute warfare at the strategic level:

Ten Strategic War Operations — 战争行动十种主要形式

§7 Island Operations 岛屿作战 Островные операции

The most strategically significant operation type — the implied Taiwan scenario. Key characteristics: multi-dimensional combat space (ground, sea, air, cyber-EM, space); diversified combat methods including large-scale conventional battles and irregular operations; extreme logistical difficulty; and fierce confrontation intensity. The text emphasizes the need for unified comprehensive support across all services.

§9 Cyber-Electromagnetic Space Warfare 网络电磁空间作战

This warfare domain "starts before other combat operations and runs through the entire process of the war." Three components: network warfare (offensive/defensive cyber operations), electronic warfare (jamming, spoofing, intercept), and information operations. The book notes that "relevant forces hidden in the national system" — including private enterprises and even skilled amateurs — are potential cyber warfare assets.

§10 The "Three Warfares" 三战 Три вида борьбы

Public opinion warfare информационная борьба, legal warfare юридическая борьба, and psychological warfare психологическая борьба form an interconnected whole. They "infiltrate each other and take advantage of each other" — all serving the overall political and military goals.

Chapter 12 · 第十二章
Battle Situation Control 战局控制

Controlling the war situation Управление ходом войны means scientifically designing and actively controlling the beginning, process, and outcome of war. Three critical control points:

1. Controlling the Opening 控制战争开局

"Mistakes at the beginning of war may continue to be magnified" throughout the conflict. The opening must be controlled with extreme caution: strictly control conditions for starting the war, limit the scope of operations, and select targets carefully — "hitting one point, shaking the whole situation."

2. Controlling Development 控制战局发展

Maintain sustained attack power and fast-paced operations in main directions. Grasp phase transitions, concentrate elite forces at critical moments, and strictly control escalation — "large-scale war caused by continuous escalation will adversely affect domestic politics, economics, and social stability."

3. Controlling the End 控制战争结局

Four situations requiring war termination:

1. War faces danger of escalation and continuing serves no benefit

2. Strategic objectives have been basically achieved

3. Continuation conflicts with national interests

4. International pressure creates unacceptable political costs

The text emphasizes: "military operations and political negotiations must be used interchangeably" — fighting and negotiating simultaneously to achieve optimal terms.

Chapter 13 · 第十三章
Operational Guidance 作战指导
"Asymmetric independent operations" and "multi-domain integrated joint operations" — the two foundational operational concepts.

Three Foundational Operational Thoughts

1. Asymmetric Independent Operations 非对称自主作战 Асимметричные самостоятельные операции

Operations that "focus on ourselves, flexibly use forces, weapons, strategies and tactics, and skillfully use the combat environment to create and maintain local or overall advantages against the enemy." The key principle: "superiority and initiative are the core elements" — always fight on your own terms, create favorable conditions, and exploit the enemy's weaknesses rather than attacking their strengths.

2. Multi-Domain Integrated Joint Operations 多域一体联合作战 Многодоменные совместные операции

All combat forces and elements integrated into a "seamlessly linked combat system through a network information system." Five key capabilities: comprehensive perception, real-time command, precision strike, full-dimensional protection, and focused support.

3. System Destruction Warfare 体系破击战 Системная война на уничтожение

The basic method of warfare: "regarding the critical system of decisive combat as the basic method." Target the enemy's command organizations, leaders, communication nodes, and information systems — destroy the enemy's "combat system" rather than trying to annihilate all forces.

Operational Guiding Principles

Five critical principles: (1) seize information superiority as the prerequisite; (2) seize air supremacy as the "decisive link and core content"; (3) seize space and network control; (4) concentrate forces at decisive points; (5) win quickly with minimal cost — "do not start the war lightly because the opponent is weak... do not be reckless because of smooth start."

A groundbreaking chapter for Chinese military doctrine — explicitly addressing overseas military operations. Key principles:

Chapter 16 · 第十六章
Strategic Guidance for Force Building 军力建设战略指导

Five Governing Laws of Force Development

1
National Security Needs
Force building must adapt to national security and development interests. Потребности безопасности
2
Economic Foundation
Military power is built on economic and technological foundations. Cannot exceed what the economy can sustain. Экономическая основа
3
War Form Evolution
Force structure must adapt to the current transition from mechanized to informatized to intelligentized warfare. Эволюция форм войны
4
Asymmetric Development

Integrate national civilian technology with military capability across "all elements, multiple domains, and high efficiency." Военно-гражданская интеграция

Key Force Building Principles

Three paramount principles: information-led (信息主导) — information technology is the core driver; system-of-systems construction (体系建设) — build integrated combat systems, not isolated platforms; and innovation-driven (创新驱动) — leverage latecomer advantages through technological leapfrogging.

Five combat capabilities to build: Comprehensive perception (全面感知), real-time command (实时指挥), precision strike (精确打击), full-dimensional protection (全维防护), and focused support (聚焦保障). These five capabilities define the target state of the PLA's integrated joint combat system.

PLA Service Branches: Construction & Development
PLA Force Structure — 中国人民解放军力量体系

Chapter 17: Army 陆军 Сухопутные войска

The army is undergoing strategic transformation from the "main position" of war to one element of joint operations. Key trends: three-dimensionalization (developing air assault and army aviation forces), modularization (following the 2004 U.S. Army model), light/rapid forces, and intelligentization — the Russian Armed Forces Chief Gerasimov is quoted: "Mankind will soon witness the robot army fighting independently."

Chapter 18: Navy 海军 Военно-морские силы

The navy is transitioning from near-seas defense to far-seas operations 近海防御到远海防卫. Key developments: carrier battle group capability, nuclear submarine fleet expansion, integrated amphibious assault capability, and "combat system with information as the main guide". The text frames naval development as essential for protecting expanding overseas interests, sea lines of communication, and maritime territorial claims.

Chapter 19: Air Force 空军 Военно-воздушные силы

The air force aims for air-space integration 空天一体 — merging air and space operations into unified combat power. Current structural problems: the offense-to-defense ratio is heavily imbalanced (1:10 in support/defense), unsuitable for modern joint operations. Needed: strategic offensive capability, stealth platforms, precision strike at extended range, and integrated air defense systems.

Chapter 20: Rocket Force 火箭军 Ракетные войска стратегического назначения

The Rocket Force is described as "the core force of China's strategic deterrence and the strategic support of China's status as a major power." Key principle: nuclear-conventional integration — combining nuclear counterattack capability with conventional precision strike into unified command. China pursues "lean and effective" (精干有效) nuclear forces — fewer warheads but reliable second-strike capability. The text explicitly discusses missile penetration technology (deception, interference, stealth, active evasion) to defeat missile defense.

On nuclear posture: China's stated policy of "No First Use" is affirmed, but the text emphasizes "ensuring effective nuclear counterattacks" and maintaining capability that makes adversaries believe any nuclear attack will result in "unacceptable retaliatory damage." Mobile operations combined with fixed positions form the operational backbone.

Chapter 21: Military Space Forces 军事航天力量 Военно-космические силы

Space forces capabilities defined in four categories: space situational awareness (tracking all objects in orbit), soft kill (jamming, blinding, cyber), hard kill (physical destruction of satellites), and space defense (protecting own assets). The text envisions space stations as "transit bases for space activities" and calls for establishing "rules of engagement for space wars" — acknowledging space as an inevitable future battlefield.

Chapter 22: Cyberspace Forces 网络空间力量 Кибервойска

Cyberspace forces encompass: reconnaissance, attack, defense, and deterrence operations. The text emphasizes "active defense" in cyberspace — not merely passive protection but proactive capability to "detect, resist, and counterattack network intrusions." Key principle: develop indigenous capability to avoid dependence on foreign technology. Cyber forces should integrate military professionals, government agencies, private sector experts, and "even some amateurs with special skills."

Chapter 23: Joint Logistics Объединённое тыловое обеспечение

The Joint Logistics Support Force represents the PLA's centralized logistics system — providing unified support across all services. Key capabilities: strategic transportation (air, sea, rail), precision logistics support, information-based supply chain management. The text emphasizes "wartime logistics must be prepared in peacetime".

Chapter 24: Armed Police Forces 武警部队 Народная вооружённая полиция

The People's Armed Police handle internal security: counter-terrorism, border security, maritime law enforcement, critical infrastructure protection, and WMD response. Under Xi Jinping's reforms, the PAP was placed under direct CMC command — removing it from the dual State Council/CMC structure.

Chapter 25: Reserve Forces 后备力量 Резервные силы

Reserve forces are transitioning from "strategic forces" (used as last resort) to "combat forces" (ready for immediate use). The U.S. model is studied extensively: National Guard civil support teams, reserve integration into active operations. Chinese reserve forces must develop: rapid mobilization, wartime support, emergency response, and specialized capabilities (communications, strategic delivery, engineering).
